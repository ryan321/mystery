The Blackwood Inheritance
☰ 🖊 ⌖

Location Blackwood Manor — the entrance hall

The manor looms against the storm. Inside, the entrance hall smells of wax and old money. A crystal vase lies shattered on the marble. Rainwater pools near the east door, and the grandfather clock has stopped at eleven. Butler Henshaw stands by the pantry door, pale and silent. When he sees you looking his way, he straightens his jacket.

You Examine the broken vase and the floor around it.

The pieces scatter outward, as if struck from above. Among the shards, a single black thread snags on the jagged edge. Near the east door, a wet footprint points toward the library — a man’s boot, size eleven, not the polished shoes the staff wear.

✦ Black thread and a muddy boot-print added to your evidence.

You turn to the butler by the pantry door.

You “Henshaw. What did you see tonight?”
B
Butler Henshaw “I heard the crash just after the clock struck eleven, sir. I was in the pantry. When I arrived, the east door stood open and Mr. Blackwood was at the top of the stairs.”
You “Who else was in the house?”
B
Butler Henshaw “Only Mrs. Blackwood, Miss Clara, and Mr. Vale, the guest. And myself, of course.”
You Follow the footprint to the library.

Location Blackwood Manor — the library

The library is cold and dark. A fire smolders in the hearth. On the desk, a ledger lies open to tonight’s date. A brass key glints in the ash.

You Take the brass key and try it on the desk drawer.

The key turns with a dry click. Inside: a letter signed by Mr. Vale, dated yesterday. “If you expose me, I will have no choice.”

✦ Vale’s letter added to your evidence.

You Return to the entrance hall and look for Mr. Vale.

Location Blackwood Manor — the entrance hall

Mr. Vale stands by the conservatory doors, adjusting his cuffs. He looks up as you approach.

You “What is this letter about, Mr. Vale?”
V
Mr. Vale He turns from the window. “Nothing. A business disagreement. I was in the conservatory all evening.”

The conservatory is on the west side — far from the east door, the broken vase, and the footprint that points away from it.

Mrs. Blackwood appears at the foot of the stairs, watching the two of you. Her gloves are dry, but her shoes are spotted with rainwater.

>

Ask Mrs. Blackwood where she was at eleven…

You write in plain language. The mystery answers.
