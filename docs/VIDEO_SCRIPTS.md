# Case Video Scripts — "One Suspect, One Lie"

**Status:** Active — scripts for the short-video channel (TikTok / Reels / Shorts)
**Date:** 2026-07-21
**Related:** [PRODUCT.md](./PRODUCT.md) §7 (audience & reader playbook)

The format: every video is a two-minute mystery the viewer participates in.
The case details are the *setting*, never the subject. The details page
tells; the video asks.

---

## The template (~60s)

| Beat | Length | Job |
|------|--------|-----|
| Crime | 4s | The hook, told as story — use the authored premise line, never a title card |
| Suspect | 8s | One name, one public fact about them |
| Live question | 10s | Type it in the real UI, no cuts; let the thinking pause breathe — don't talk over it |
| Their story | 12s | The reply exactly as a real run produced it |
| The thing that doesn't fit | 12s | Freeze-frame, one clue, hard sound cue |
| Open verdict | 12s | Never accuse — ask. All suspects on screen. "First case free, link in bio." |

**Caption formula:** his story / the contradiction / "Suspect N of M — who
would you press next?" / first case free + `#mystery #whodunit #detective
#booktok #cozymystery #puzzle`

**Pinned comment (always, immediately):** "Rules of the case: every clue
you need is findable, the solution never changes, no trick endings. Wrong
guesses welcome — the town already made one." *(The fair-play promise doing
marketing work; seeds the debate thread.)*

## Production rules

- **Real footage only.** Screen-record the actual play UI (mobile layout,
  9:16), typed live. The thinking pause and the in-character answer are the
  differentiator; After-Effects polish makes it look like every other game
  trailer. Whatever line the performer gives that day is the take.
- **Case ambience under everything** (each theme ships its own music).
- Founder voiceover, quiet and unhurried. Text overlays echo the VO's key
  phrases, never paraphrase them.
- Batch per case: one recording session yields the whole suspect series.

## Spoiler rules (non-negotiable)

- A script may use only **pre-start surfaces** (premise, briefing, cast
  cards — the same audit-protected material as the details page) plus **at
  most one early clue**.
- **Never the villain's load-bearing lie.** The killer's episode must be
  the *least* accusatory of the series.
- Never mid-game reversals (Blackwood: Clara's confession beat; Pier: the
  reorientations). Marketing spends the question, never the answer.
- The video never claims X is the killer — one verified evasion, meaning
  left open. That keeps comments a debate, not a spoiler thread.

## PIER AT LOW TIDE — Episode 1 of 5: "The Deckhand" (Jess Pike)

*Safe because the case's own pre-start surfaces point deflection at the
public quarrel.*

**[0:00–0:04] — THE HOOK**
Screen: Pier cover art, slow push-in. Fog. One lantern.
VO: "A harbormaster is dead under his own pier. The whole town says the
tide took him."
Overlay: `THE TOWN SAYS: ACCIDENT.`

**[0:05–0:12] — YOUR JOB**
Screen: real play UI; briefing card for a beat, then the composer.
VO: "I'm the inspector. My job is to sign the death certificate and go
home. But one man fought with the victim yesterday — in front of everyone."
Overlay: `JESS PIKE — DECKHAND. LAST MAN TO FIGHT HIM.`

**[0:13–0:22] — ASK HIM YOURSELF**
Screen: type live, no cuts: *"People say you and Rourke nearly came to
blows yesterday. What was the fight about?"* Send. Thinking beat. Portrait
slides in.
VO: silence. The pause is the tension.

**[0:23–0:35] — THE ANSWER**
Screen: Jess's reply as the run produced it (expected shape: wages,
defiance, "I'd have said it to his face again this morning").
VO (over the tail): "Wages. That's his story."
Overlay: `HIS STORY: WAGES.`

**[0:36–0:47] — THE CATCH**
Screen: evidence drawer, one item highlighted, freeze-frame, slow zoom.
Hard sound cue.
VO: "Except three people watched that fight. And not one of them heard
anybody mention money."
Overlay: `NOBODY HEARD 'WAGES.'` *(beat)* `SO WHAT WAS THE FIGHT ABOUT?`

**[0:48–0:60] — OPEN VERDICT**
Screen: composer, cursor blinking. Cast strip: five faces.
VO: "He's lying about the fight. But lying about a fight isn't the same as
killing a man. Five people were on that pier before dawn. Tell me who you'd
press next — and if you think you can read a liar better than I can, the
first case is free. Link in bio."
Overlay: `WHO DO YOU PRESS NEXT?` → `NELL · BRIGGS · JESS · DR. COLE · SAM`
End card: logo + `First case free · mysterytrove.com`

**Series map (Pier):** ep2 Nell (she found him — and greeted someone in the
fog), ep3 Sam (on watch all night, saw nothing), ep4 Dr. Cole (walks the
pier at night; counted the minutes), ep5 Briggs (his boat was moored out —
keep this one the gentlest of the five).

## BLACKWOOD INHERITANCE — Episode 1: "The Guest" (Mr. Vale)

*Safe because Vale is the authored red herring with an honest cause, and
the landing page's public sample play already ships this exact beat (the
letter, the conservatory alibi). The briefing's own deflection target —
"at dawn they take Garrick" — frames the hook.*

**[0:00–0:05] — THE HOOK**
Screen: manor art in the storm — rain, lightning flash. Then the stopped
clock.
VO: "A storm-locked manor. The master of the house dead at the foot of the
stairs. And a grandfather clock stopped at seven minutes past eleven."
Overlay: `THE CLOCK STOPPED AT 11:07.`

**[0:06–0:14] — YOUR JOB**
Screen: real play UI; briefing card, then the composer.
VO: "At first light, the police take the caretaker, and everyone in the
county will call it justice. I have until dawn to find out if they're
wrong. And there's a guest in this house with a letter he hoped nobody
would find."
Overlay: `AT DAWN THEY ARREST THE CARETAKER.` → `UNLESS.`

**[0:15–0:24] — ASK HIM YOURSELF**
Screen: type live, no cuts: *"Where were you when the vase broke,
Mr. Vale?"* Send. Thinking beat. Vale's portrait slides in.
VO: silence through the pause.

**[0:25–0:36] — THE ANSWER**
Screen: Vale's reply as the run produced it (expected shape: adjusting his
cuffs, "a business disagreement," "I was in the conservatory all evening").
VO (over the tail): "The conservatory. All evening. That's his story."
Overlay: `HIS STORY: THE CONSERVATORY.`

**[0:37–0:49] — THE CATCH**
Screen: evidence drawer — the letter, freeze-frame, zoom on the line.
Thunder sting.
VO: "Except locked in the dead man's desk there's a letter, signed by
Mr. Vale, dated the day before the murder. It says: 'If you expose me, I
will have no choice.'"
Overlay: `"IF YOU EXPOSE ME, I WILL HAVE NO CHOICE."` *(beat)* `NO CHOICE
BUT WHAT?`

**[0:50–0:62] — OPEN VERDICT**
Screen: composer, cursor blinking. Cast strip across the bottom.
VO: "A threat isn't a confession — desperate men write desperate letters.
But somebody in this house pushed a man down those stairs, and the whole
household was awake to hear it. Tell me who you'd question first — and if
you think you'd have this solved by dawn, the first case is free. Link in
bio."
Overlay: `WHO DO YOU QUESTION FIRST?` → `THE WIDOW · THE BUTLER · THE
GUEST · THE DAUGHTER · THE CARETAKER…`
End card: logo + `First case free · mysterytrove.com`

**Series map (Blackwood):** rich — thirteen living characters. Safe
subjects for further episodes: Henshaw (heard the crash from the pantry),
Mrs. Hollis (judges all humanity by appetite; what did the kitchen see?),
Constance (novels hidden in sermon covers), Poppy (the nine-year-old who
notices everything), the incident book itself ("six weeks of break-ins
that never climbed the stairs" — a clue episode, no suspect). **Off-limits
as episode subjects:** Mrs. Marsh beyond background scenery, Clara's
confession beat, and Garrick beyond the "obvious man" framing the briefing
already sells.
